The user performs a hand gesture, such as a pinch in and pinch out, while watching a video. This gesture serves as an input signal for controlling the volume. Gesture Detection: The system utilizes sensors or cameras to detect the hand gesture in real-time. These sensors capture the spatial movements and positions of the user's hand.

03 Gesture Recognition:

Once detected, the system applies gesture recognition algorithms to interpret the hand gesture. This involves analyzing the direction, speed, and intensity of the gesture to determine the intended volume control action. Volume Adjustment: Based on the recognized gesture, the system adjusts the audio volume accordingly. For example, a pinch out gesture may trigger an increase in volume, while a pinch in gesture may trigger a decrease.

The system continues to monitor for additional gestures, allowing users to adjust the volume seamlessly throughout the video playback experience. After detecting and interpreting the initial hand gesture for volume control, the Zeptune system continues to actively monitor for additional gestures throughout the video playback session. This continuous monitoring ensures that users can adjust the volume as needed at any point during the video playback experience, providing a seamless and responsive interaction.
